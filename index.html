fern denne

ABOUT

Highlighting moments of discovery and connection in public parks to illustrate the importance of community-oriented natural environments.

You are Here is a visual exploration led by the belief that interactions with parks inform one’s future relationships with nature. How do we foster these relationships with care and appreciation for safe, community-oriented spaces inspiring curiosity and reflection? By emphasizing micro-moments of connection between people, places, plants, and creatures, You are Here invites you to take a closer look at the world around you.

PROBLEM STATEMENT

Everyone deserves access to high-quality, publicly owned green space; a community-oriented environment to interact with nature safely and curiously.

But in 2010 (the most recent data currently available), only 39% of the total U.S. population lived within a half-mile of a park. So how do we highlight the importance of public parks, for the sake of environmental education, community gathering, and personal reflection?

Do parks inform people’s future interactions with nature?

RESEARCH TOWARDS A SOLUTION

“The use of parks has been linked specifically to increased physical activity, stronger social integration, reduced stress and anxiety, and overall improvements in health (Bedimo-Rung, Mowen, & Cohen, 2005; Sallis et al., 2015). [...] Parks with shade trees also provide environmental benefits such as storing stormwater, filtering water and air pollutants, and cooling urban heat islands, thereby reducing air pollution exposure and preventing heat-related illness among park users or nearby residents (Bedimo-Rung et al., 2005)”.

This project aims to showcase the ways that public parks in St Louis, a city highly concentrated with parks (94% of the population lives within half a mile of a park), enrich our lives.
